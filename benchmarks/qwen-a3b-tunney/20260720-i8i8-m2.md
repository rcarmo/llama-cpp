# Qwen dense i8×i8 m2 kernel

Date: 20 July 2026

`GGML_RISCV64_SPACEMIT_I8I8_M2=1` enables a two-row IME2 kernel for dense Q8 verification tails. Dispatch uses m4→m2→m1 decomposition. The m2 kernel loads and unpacks each 32×32 B tile once, applies it to two adjacent independently quantised activation rows, and keeps two FP32 output accumulators in vector registers.

Correctness passed for activation rows 1–9 and 32, plus K=256 and K=1024 edge cases, at one and eight GGML threads. Gate-off and gate-on outputs were identical.

Focused eight-thread timings, 1,000 repeated computes:

| Activation rows | Existing m4/m1 family, µs | m4/m2/m1 family, µs | Gain |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2 | 25.901 | 24.978 | 3.6% |
| 3 | 29.008 | 28.101 | 3.1% |
| 6 | 31.253 | 29.995 | 4.0% |
| 7 | 33.450 | 32.385 | 3.2% |

Generated assembly contains no vector spills. B is loaded with two `vl4r.v` operations per K block and shared across the two A-row dot products. Results remain in `v2` and `v3` until the final `vse32.v` stores. Scalar stack traffic is limited to the function prologue/epilogue.

Promotion still requires an end-to-end Qwen MTP win beyond the 2% noise threshold without reduced draft acceptance.
